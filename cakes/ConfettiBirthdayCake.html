Confetti Birthday Cake

I really enjoyed making this cake for mine and my friend's birthday. It's so colorful and fun, and tasted great. I love cream cheese frosting which is why I chose to top this cake with it, but I'm sure a whipped or iced topping would pair perfectly with it too.
Made by Lavi
Total Time
4 hours
Servings
20

Ingredients for the Cake

1 1/2 cups
Buttermilk
4 cups
All-purpose flour
2 tsp
Baking Powder
1 tsp
Baking Soda
A Pinch
Salt
2 1/2 cups
Granulated Sugar
1 cup
Unsalted Butter (softened)
4
Eggs
4
Eggs Yolks
1/2 cup
Vegetable Oil
2 tsp
Vanilla Extract
1 1/2 cup
Rainbow Sugar Sprinkles

Ingredients for the Frosting

16 oz
Cream Cheese
1/2 cup
Unsalted Butter (softened)
2 tsp
Vanilla
4 1/2 cups
Powdered Sugar

Directions for the Cake

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees, and grease three cake pans. Then combine the buttermilk and vegetable oil, and set aside.
  2. Sift and whisk the flour, baking powder, and baking soda in a separate bowl, mix in the salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the sugar and butter until creamy. Then incrementally add in the eggs, yolks, and vanilla while beating.
  4. On low speed, alternate adding in the flour mixture and oil mixture. Beat until well-incorporated.
  5. Fold in 3/4 cups of the sprinkles, and then evenly distribute the batter in the pans and bake for 30 minutes.
  6. When done, invert each cake and make sure to cool completely before icing. I waited about 2 hours.

Directions for the Frosting

  1. For the frosting, beat the cream cheese and butter until creamy. Then slowly add the powdered sugar and vanilla, mixing continuously on high speed for about 2 minutes.
  2. When the cakes are finished cooling, place the first cake on the serving plate, and then put an even layer of frosting. Repeat this for the second and third cakes. Spread the remaining frosting on the sides of the cake, and optionally decorate with the remainder of the sprinkles.
  3. Chill the cake in the fridge until ready to serve.
