Info

Made this for my parents anniversary! It’s a chocolate cake with a layer of ganache and raspberries topped with a chocolate buttercream frosting!
Made by Maryam
Total Time
1 hour
Servings
9-12

Ingredients for the Cake

2 cups
All-purpose Flour
1 1/2 cups
Granulated Sugar
3/4 cup
Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
2 tsp
Baking Powder
1 1/2 tsp
Baking Soda
1 tsp
Salt
1 tsp
Instant Coffee or Espresso Powder
1 cup
Almond Milk
1/2 cup
Vegetable Oil
2
Eggs
2 tsp
Vanilla Extract
3/4 cup
Boiling Water
3 packets
Raspberries

Ingredients for the Ganache

1/2 bar
Dark Chocolate
1/4 cup
Almond Milk

Ingredients for the Buttercream

1 cup
Vegan Butter
1/2 cup
Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
1/3 cup
Almond Milk
1 tbsp
Vanilla Extract
2 cups
Powdered Sugar

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees and spray two 10" springform pans with oil. Starting with the cake mix, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, salt and dry coffee.
  2. Next mix in wet ingredients, milk, oil, eggs and vanilla using a mixer on slow speed. Once combined, add in boiling water to thin out the consistency! This will make the cake extra moist.
  3. Pour the batter evenly into the two pans and bake for 30-35 minutes. While your cakes are cooling, wash the raspberries and prepare the ganache. To make the ganache, melt chocolate in the microwave with milk in 30 second increments.
  4. Take the bottom layer cake and spread chocolate ganache over the top. Add a layer of raspberries on top of the ganache (shown in the picture). Add the next cake layer on top. Next prepare the buttercream.
  5. To make the buttercream, mix the butter and cocoa powder on low speed until fluffy. Add in milk and vanilla and mix.
  6. Finally, slowly add in powdered sugar until you reach desired taste. Pipe buttercream on the top layer of the cake and decorate with raspberries! Enjoy!
  • Note: It helps if the cakes are level for stacking so you might need to shave off the top. I did not do this for the bottom layer and used the side that wasn’t level as the bottom so I could layer the ganache and raspberries which is another way to do it.
