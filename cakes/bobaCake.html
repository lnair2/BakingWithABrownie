Info

For Milk Tea and Boba lovers everywhere, this is the cake to make! This was definitely a very time consuming cake to make because there were so many different parts that went into it, but it had a very nice strong tea flavor which was delicious.
Really important note!! If you are adding the boba pearls to the top of the cake, add them right before serving. The boba will harden into a really unpleasant consistency if you cool it with the boba.

Directions for the Cake

  1. Pre-heat oven to 350Â°, and lightly grease 3 cake pans with butter and flour.
  2. Bring milk to a simmer, and add the tea and let steep for 30 minutes.
  3. Squeese and strain out the tea. Measure remaining liquid. Add additional milk until you have 3/4 cup liquids.
  4. In a medium bowl whisk together 2 cups flour (reserve remaining 1/2 cup), sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Slowly add in the butter, and continue to mix on low speed until mixture is crumbly.
  6. In separate bowl, combine the tea milk, eggs, and egg white. Add half of the liquid to the cake mixture and mix until incorporated.
  7. Add remaining half of the liquid and mix until batter is combined. Add the rest of the 1/2 cup flour and mix until combined.
  8. Divide batter into prepared pans. Bake until golden (use toothpick to test for doneness), approximately 30-35 minutes.
  9. After letting the cakes cool for a bit, invert the cakes onto wire racks to let them cool completely.

Directions for Tea Syrup

  1. Bring water and sugar to a boil.
  2. Put on low heat and steep tea bags for 30 minutes.
  3. Cool and store in the fridge until ready to use.

Directions for Milk Tea Concentrate

  1. Simmer milk, and then lower from heat.
  2. Steep tea bags for 20 minutes.
  3. Squeeze and remove tea bags, and place mixture in the fridge until ready to use.

Directions for Milk Tea Buttercream

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter until creamy.
  2. Add powdered sugar and beat until well combined.
  3. Mix in vanilla, salt, and Milk Tea Concentrate until incorporated.

Directions for Cake Syrup

  1. Combine Tea Syrup and Milk Tea Concentrate.

Directions for Boba (This step should only occur before serving the cake)

  1. These steps vary on what type of boba you have, usually there are directions on the package.
  2. Typically just boil 5 cups of water for your 1 cup of boba.
  3. After the water starts boiling, add in the boba and let it cook until the boba looks shiny and squishy.
  4. Lower the heat and continue cooking until boba is soft and chewy. Add the brown sugar and cook until sugar dissolves.
  5. Remove from heat, and let cool until the syrup thickens a bit.

Assembling the Cake

  1. Place down the first cake, and brush 1/3 of the Cake Syrup on top of it. Then spread a layer of the buttercream.
  2. Repeat the first step for the other 2 cakes.
  3. Spread the remaining buttercream around the exterior of the cake.
  4. Chill the cake for about 20 minutes.
  5. Add the warm boba to the top of the cake just before serving.
