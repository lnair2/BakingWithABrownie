Info

I was really surprised at how easy this Panna Cotta was to make. It was so creamy and delicious, and I will definitely be trying out different toppings and sauces to pair with it!
Made by Lavi
Total Time
5 hours and 30 minutes (including cooling time)
Servings
6 ramekins

Ingredients for Panna Cotta

1 Envelope
Unflavored Gelatin
2 tbsp
Water
2 cups
Heavy Whipping Cream
1 cup
Half and Half
1/4 cup
Granulated Sugar
2 tsp
Vanilla Extract

Ingredients for Berry Sauce

A Pint
Fresh Raspberries
1 tsp
Corn Starch
2 tbsp
Raspberry Jam
1 tbsp
Lemon Juice

Directions

  1. In a saucepan, pour the water over the gelatin, and stir over low heat. When the gelatin is completely dissolved, remove from heat.
  2. In another saucepan, bring the half and half, heavy whipping cream, and sugar to a boil. Then stir in the gelatin and the vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the mixture equally in each of your ramekins, and then chill for at least 4 hours (the longer the better).
  4. For the sauce, add the lemon juice and raspberries to a sauce pan, and bring to a boil. Add the corn starch and jam to thicken the mixture, stirring continuously.
  5. Strain the sauce to remove any raspberry seeds.
  6. When the panna cotta is finished cooling, dip the bottom of the ramekins in hot water and run a knife around the edges to loosen them. Invert them onto plates and spoon the sauce over each serving. Enjoy!

    7. *Note: I prefer my desserts to be more tart, so feel free to add some sugar to the berry sauce to sweeten it up!
