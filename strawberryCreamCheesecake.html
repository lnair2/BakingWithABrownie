Info

We love this cheesecake so much we made it twice in one week, couldn't get enough! It takes a bit of time to make, but is so worth it. It's a tad on the sweet side, so feel free to adjust the sugar to your preference.
Total Time
7 hours (including cooling time)
Servings
10

Ingredients for the Cake

1 1/4 lb
Strawberries
3 tbsp
Light Corn Syrup
4 sheets
Graham Crackers
1 cup and 4tbs.
Sugar
3 tbs
Strawbery Jam
3 tbsp
Butter (unsalted melted)
1 lb and 13oz
Cream Cheese
A Pinch
Salt
2
Eggs
1 tsp
Vanilla Extract
8 oz
Mascarpone Cheese

Directions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 300°, and place 1 lb of the strawberries onto a rimmed baking sheet.
  2. Drizzle the jam and corn syrup on top of the strawberries, and toss to coat.
  3. Bake for about 1 hour and 20 minutes or until a thick syrup forms, and strawberries shrivel.
  4. Transfer strawberries to a bowl and mash up. Set aside to cool completely.
  5. Raise temperature to 350°. Crush the graham crackers, add 4 tbs of sugar, and the butter.
  6. Mix until combened and then press into a greased springform pan. Bake crust for 9 minutes or until firm. Let crust cool completely.
  7. Reduce oven to 325°, and put cream cheese into a large bowl and mix until creamy.
  8. Add the sugar and salt and continue mixing. Then gradually add in the eggs.
  9. Add the vanilla and mascarpone cheese. Continue mixing until creamy and well-combined.
  10. Transfer 2/3 of the cream cheese mixture to the mashed strawberries.
  11. Pour the strawberry mixture onto the crust, and then carefully spoon and spread the cream cheese mixture on top.
  12. Wrap the springform pan's exterior in aluminum foil, and place into a roasting pan. Fill with boiling water until it reaches halfway to the pan.
  13. Bake cheesecake for 1 hour and 20 minutes. The cheesecake should be slighly jiggly when lighly shaken.
  14. Place on a wire rack to cool, and then chill completely in the fridge for at least 5 hours or overnight.
  15. Decorate with remaining strawberries before serving.
