Lemon Merengue Pie

My dad isn't a huge fan of cake, so for his birthday I made a lemon merengue birthday pie instead! We love our desserts a little more on the tart side, so I added more lemon juice and zest to it. For the crust, I used the graham cracker ingredients and directions from the Key Lime Pie recipe which can be found here: Key Lime Pie Recipe.
Made by Lavi
Total Time
2 and 1/2 hours (includes cooling time)
Servings
12

Ingredients for the Filling

1 cup
Granulated Sugar
2 tbs
All-purpose Flour
3 tbs
Cornstarch
3 tbs
Unsalted Butter
4
Egg Yolks
A Pinch
Salt
1 1/2 cups
Water
1/2 or 3/4 cup
Fresh lemon juice (about 2 1/2 lemons)
Approx. 2 tbs
zest from 2 1/2 lemons

Ingredients for the Merenge

4
Egg Whites
1/3 cup
Granulated Sugar

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees, and stir sugar, flour, cornstarch, and salt in a sauce pan.
  2. Mix in the water, lemon juice and zest and begin cooking the mixture on medium heat.
  3. Stir continuously and mix in the butter once the mixture comes to a boil.
  4. Whisk the egg yolks, and then slowly add to the saucepan, stirring continuously until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  5. Pour the mixture into the baked graham cracker crust.
  6. For the merengue, beat the egg whites on high until stiff peaks form. Incrementally add the sugar to the mixture, beating continuously.
  7. Spread the merengue over the pie, and then bake for 9 minutes.
  8. Chill for a couple hours before enjoying.
